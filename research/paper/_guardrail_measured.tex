\subsection{Specialized guards under our controlled protocol}
\label{sec:guardrail-measured}

The published numbers above are self-reported and not comparable across models (\S\ref{sec:guardrail-landscape}). To put a subset on a controlled footing, we re-evaluate seven specialized guards on the \emph{identical} frozen rows and protocol used for our own guard. Three findings, all from controlled measurement. \textbf{(1) Out-of-distribution ranking.} On the novel benchmarks the open guards order as untuned base (0.886) $>$ Llama-Guard-3-8B (0.833) $>$ our tuned guard (0.781) $>$ Llama-Guard-3-1B (0.701): the 8B guard is a strong OOD \emph{ranker} --- second only to our untuned base --- yet at the deployable matched-FPR@0.10 operating point our 3B guard leads it on F1 (0.581 vs 0.475) at a quarter of the VRAM, because AUPRC rewards ranking while the guards must still be read at a threshold. \textbf{(2) Over-blocking is universal among the strong recall-first guards.} Qwen3Guard-4B and WildGuard-7B both post high recall ($\approx$0.93) but heavy false-positive rates on OR-Bench-hard (0.81 and 0.73) --- an independent, cross-vendor confirmation of the over-refusal failure mode this paper isolates (\S\ref{sec:mortgage-casestudy}). \textbf{(3) A specialized injection detector is not a general moderator.} Prompt Guard 2-86M scores 0.903 AUPRC on our in-house injection subsets (its designed task) but only 0.625 on the novel jailbreak sets, and by construction does not classify general harm --- so it is complementary to, not a replacement for, a content guard.

\subsection{Recommendations}
\label{sec:recommendations}

Combining our controlled measurements with the published landscape and deployment profiles, and scoped to \emph{input-prompt} moderation (our evaluation setting; response- and tool-moderation are out of scope, \S\ref{sec:future}):

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Best overall guard (accuracy-first, resources permitting).} No single winner: Llama-Guard-3-8B is the strongest measured OOD ranker among off-the-shelf guards (novel AUPRC 0.833), and Qwen3Guard reports the strongest published generalist profile; both cost 8--16\,GB and 0.5--0.9\,s per call and over-block. If a frontier API is acceptable, gpt-5.4-mini ties our guard on F1.
  \item \textbf{Best lightweight base to fine-tune.} If in-house accuracy and a low false-positive operating point dominate, \textbf{Qwen3-4B} --- the highest measured in-house AUPRC (0.882) and matched-FPR@0.10 F1 (0.753) of the six bases, at 4B / 112\,ms (\Cref{tab:slm-sweep}); Qwen3-8B is statistically indistinguishable at double the memory. If efficiency dominates, any 1.5--3B base (SmolLM2, Qwen2.5, SmolLM3) reaches in-house AUPRC 0.82--0.85 at a fraction of the VRAM. Because tuning \emph{converges} out-of-distribution behaviour across bases (\Cref{sec:cross-family}), the base barely affects OOD robustness --- so choose on in-house accuracy, cost, and license, not on hoped-for generalization; and where OOD robustness is paramount, keep the untuned base in a base$\oplus$tuned ensemble (\Cref{sec:ensemble}).
  \item \textbf{Best prompt-injection / jailbreak detector.} Prompt Guard 2-86M --- 0.903 measured in-house-injection AUPRC, 0.998 published jailbreak AUC, 0.56\,GB, sub-100\,ms; pair it with a content guard rather than using it alone.
  \item \textbf{Best content-category moderation.} ShieldGemma (published OpenAI-Mod F1 0.82) for policy-category outputs; but on our hardened domain set every small guard over-blocks (\S\ref{sec:mortgage-casestudy}), so category moderation still needs an operating-point audit.
  \item \textbf{Best performance/efficiency balance.} Our laptop-trained 3B guard: it leads all measured open guards in-house (AUPRC 0.844) and at matched-FPR (F1 0.581) at 6\,GB / 124\,ms, and ties gpt-5.4-mini on F1; the untuned base or a base$\oplus$tuned ensemble (\S\ref{sec:ensemble}) is preferable when out-of-distribution robustness dominates.
  \item \textbf{Best for real-time production.} For pure injection/jailbreak gating, Prompt Guard 2 (encoder, single forward pass, sub-100\,ms). For general prompt moderation, Llama-Guard-3-1B (20\,ms, 3\,GB) or our 3B guard (124\,ms) --- the generative 4--8B guards (0.5--0.9\,s) are hard to justify inline. Qwen3Guard-Stream (token-level, published) is the one design here built for streaming.
\end{itemize}
