\subsection{The specialized-guardrail landscape}
\label{sec:guardrail-landscape}

Beyond the open guards we measure under our matched protocol (Llama-Guard-3-1B, ShieldGemma-2b), a fast-moving ecosystem of purpose-built guardrail models now spans three tasks --- content-category moderation, prompt-injection/jailbreak detection, and one-stop harm+refusal classification --- across two orders of magnitude in size (\Cref{tab:guardrail-specs}). We summarize this landscape from each model's \emph{published, self-reported} results (\Cref{tab:guardrail-published}) and then state plainly why those numbers cannot be read as a leaderboard.

\paragraph{Best \emph{claimed} per benchmark (self-reported; not controlled).}
On self-reported numbers, ShieldGemma-9B leads OpenAI-Mod (F1 0.821)~\cite{zeng2024shieldgemma}; Qwen3Guard-4B leads ToxicChat (0.828)~\cite{qwen3guard2025}; WildGuard-7B and Qwen3Guard-8B tie on WildGuardTest ($\approx$0.889)~\cite{han2024wildguard,qwen3guard2025}; Qwen3Guard-8B leads HarmBench, XSTest, BeaverTails, and Aegis~\cite{qwen3guard2025}; and Prompt Guard 2-86M dominates the (distinct) prompt-injection task (AUC 0.998)~\cite{meta2025promptguard2}. Qwen3Guard reports the strongest recent generalist profile --- top score on 8 of 14 English benchmarks, with even its 0.6B variant rivaling models $10\times$ larger~\cite{qwen3guard2025}.

\paragraph{Why this is not a leaderboard.}
Two concrete artifacts make cross-row comparison unsafe. First, \textbf{protocol drift}: WildGuard's own OpenAI-Mod F1 is 0.721~\cite{han2024wildguard}, but ShieldGemma's \emph{re-run} of WildGuard reports 0.779~\cite{zeng2024shieldgemma} --- a 0.058 swing from evaluation choices alone. Second, \textbf{incomparable internal sets}: Llama Guard 4's headline F1 (0.61) is on a deliberately harder/expanded benchmark and is reported by Meta only as a \emph{delta} over Llama Guard 3, so reading it against the 3-8B's 0.939 would be a category error~\cite{meta2025llamaguard4}. This is precisely the operating-point-and-benchmark fragility this paper is about (\S\ref{sec:operating-point-fairness}), now visible \emph{across} published guards. The only rows we can place on a controlled footing are the ones we re-evaluate ourselves under one protocol (\Cref{tab:main-inhouse}).
