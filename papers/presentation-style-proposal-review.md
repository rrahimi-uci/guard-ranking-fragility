# Editor's review: `presentation-style-proposal.md` vs. the current report

## 1. Overall assessment

The proposal is structurally sound and still the right plan — its three-persona / layered-disclosure / evidence-tiering thesis is exactly what this report needs, and most of its craft prescriptions (result-module grammar, evidence badges, page budget discipline, no-pooling rules) remain correct. But it was written against a build that no longer exists: since then the repo gained a **fully-run preregistered confirmatory adaptation study** (`sec:adaptation`, RQ1 supported / RQ2 not supported), and a `report.md` critique pass already applied ~7 of its P0 corrections. **The single most important thing to change now is to re-baseline the proposal's foundational premise:** delete every "the report is all-retrospective / the purpose-built study's results do not yet exist" statement and give the confirmatory study a first-class slot in the narrative spine, page budget, evidence-badge taxonomy, and source-migration map. Everything downstream (badges, cards, selector, cut order, conclusion) inherits that one stale premise, so fixing it is load-bearing for the rest.
